Moderna




Introduction:

The Moderna vaccine also known as mRNA-1273, manufactured by ModernaTX, Inc. There are four types of Covid-19 vaccines throughout the world. The Moderna is a type of mRNA vaccine.

Moderna, Inc is an American pharmaceutical and biotechnology company based in Cambridge, Massachusetts. It focuses on drug discovery, drug development, and vaccine technologies based on messenger RNA(mRNA).

In November 2020, it was announced that the Moderna COVID-19 vaccine candidate, mRNA-1273, had shown preliminary evidence of 94% efficacy in preventing COVID-19 disease in a Phase lll trial, with only minor flue-like effects.

This led to its submission for emergency use authorization in EUrope, the United States and Canada.


Here is how it works


The WHO Strategic Advisory Group of Experts (SAGE) on Immunization has issued Interim recommendations for use of the Moderna mRNA-1273 vaccine against COVID-19 in people aged 18 years and older.


Here is what you need to know.


Who should be vaccinated first?

Health workers at high risk of exposure and the elderly, as with all COVID-19 vaccines, should be vaccinated first.


Additional priority groups should be vaccinated as more vaccine becomes available, with a focus on people who are disproportionately affected by COVID-19 or who face health inequities.


Is it safe?

While the vaccine has not yet been approved by WHO for an Emergency Use Listing, it has been reviewed by the European Medicines Agency (EMA) and thus meets WHO's SAGE criteria.


The European Medicines Agency (EMA) has thoroughly reviewed the data on the Moderna COVID-19 vaccine's quality, safety, and efficacy and has approved its use throughout the European Union.


All vaccinees should be observed for at least 15 minutes after vaccination, according to SAGE. Additional doses should not be given to those who have a severe allergic reaction to the first dose.


How efficacious is the vaccine?

The Moderna vaccine has been shown to protect against COVID-19 for up to 14 days after the first dose, with an efficacy of around 92 percent.


Possible side effects




In the arm where you got the shot:

  • Pain
  • Redness
  • Swelling

Throughout the rest of your body:

  • Tiredness
  • Headache
  • Muscle pain
  • Chills
  • Fever
  • Nausea
